## PAGE 3 — The serving ritual

**Heading:** Read the rum first

**Body:**

> Before any cocktail, taste the spirit on its own. It is forty per cent ABV, designed to be read like a single malt, not knocked back like a shot.
>
> The ritual takes thirty seconds. Do it once. After that you know what is in the bottle, and every drink you build from it is informed.

**Subhead:** The glass

> A short tumbler. Heavy base, wide mouth, not chilled. The wide mouth lets the aromatics open. The heavy base keeps the rum from warming too fast in your hand.

**Subhead:** The pour

> Twenty-five millilitres. Half a standard measure. Enough to taste, not enough to commit.

**Subhead:** The three sips

> *First sip:* small. Notice the warmth at the front of the palate. Vanilla, then cinnamon. Resist the urge to swallow quickly.
>
> *Second sip:* slightly larger. Look for what sits underneath. Bourbon oak, clove, a thread of ginger. The agave gives a soft sweetness, not a sugar one.
>
> *Third sip:* the finish. How long does it stay with you. Is it warm or hot. Spiced rum should warm; it should not burn.

**Subhead:** What to expect

> A spirit that opens with vanilla and orange peel, builds through cinnamon and clove, and finishes on bourbon oak. Not sweet. Not harsh. Long.
>
> Now you know what is in the bottle. Build whichever drink you like next.

## PAGE 4 — Recipe 1: The Old Standard

**One-line lead:** The serve that shows you what the rum actually is.

**Glass:** Short tumbler. Single large ice cube.

**Ingredients:**
- 50ml Expedition Spiced Rum
- 5ml demerara syrup (1:1 demerara sugar dissolved in hot water)
- 2 dashes Angostura bitters
- Strip of orange peel, expressed

**Method:**

1. Add the syrup and bitters to a chilled mixing glass.
2. Pour the rum over. Add ice. Stir for thirty seconds.
3. Strain over one large cube in a tumbler.
4. Express the orange peel over the surface. Run the peel around the rim of the glass. Drop the peel in.

**Notes:** The Old Standard is our take on the Rum Old Fashioned. We use less sugar than most builds because Expedition carries its own sweetness from agave and vanilla. Demerara, not white. The molasses notes line up with what is already in the spirit.

**Source:** Full notes at jerrycanspirits.co.uk/field-manual/cocktails/the-old-standard

## PAGE 5 — Recipe 2: Storm & Spice

**One-line lead:** Our Dark and Stormy. Built tall, built honest.

**Glass:** Highball. Cubed ice, filled to the top.

**Ingredients:**
- 50ml Expedition Spiced Rum
- 10ml fresh lime juice
- 100ml ginger beer (the spicy kind, not the soft drink)
- Lime wedge

**Method:**

1. Fill the highball with cubed ice.
2. Add the rum and lime juice.
3. Top with ginger beer. Stir once with a bar spoon to lift the rum off the bottom.
4. Garnish with a lime wedge on the rim.

**Notes:** Ginger beer is the build. Ginger ale is a softer version. Ask the guest which they prefer. Lime is non-negotiable; the acidity stops the drink from turning sweet. If you cannot find a properly spicy ginger beer, leave the drink alone and pour the rum neat instead. A weak ginger beer kills the build.

**Source:** Full notes at jerrycanspirits.co.uk/field-manual/cocktails/storm-and-spice

## PAGE 6 — Recipe 3: Explorers Gold

**One-line lead:** Rum and honey. The combination most people have not tried.

**Glass:** Coupe or small tumbler, no ice.

**Ingredients:**
- 50ml Expedition Spiced Rum
- 15ml honey syrup (2:1 honey to hot water, stirred until clear)
- 15ml fresh lemon juice
- Lemon twist

**Method:**

1. Add the rum, honey syrup, and lemon juice to a shaker with ice.
2. Shake hard for fifteen seconds.
3. Double-strain into a chilled coupe or small tumbler.
4. Express a lemon twist over the surface. Drop it in.

**Notes:** Honey and spiced rum is an old combination most modern menus have forgotten. The honey carries the vanilla and orange notes; the lemon stops it turning syrupy. Use a strong honey if you have it. The dilution from shaking matters more than people think; under-shake and the drink is heavy.

**Source:** Full notes at jerrycanspirits.co.uk/field-manual/cocktails/explorers-gold-rum-and-honey
