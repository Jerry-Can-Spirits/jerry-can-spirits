Ways to Drink It

Most spiced rum is built to be mixed. This one is built to be sipped. Neat, over ice, or in a cocktail. It holds up in all of them.

Easy Recipes
Storm & Spice
Our take on a Dark & Stormy · 2 mins
Ingredients
50mlExpedition Spiced Rum
150mlPremium ginger beer
½Lime, squeezed
To serveIce, lime wedge
Method
  1. 1.Fill a highball glass to the top with ice.
  2. 2.Pour in the ginger beer and squeeze over the lime.
  3. 3.Float the rum on top. Pour slowly over the back of a spoon to keep it sitting proud.
  4. 4.Garnish with a lime wedge and serve without stirring.
Jerry Can Julep
Our take on a Mint Julep · 4 mins
Ingredients
50mlExpedition Spiced Rum
15mlSugar syrup
10–12Fresh mint leaves
To serveCrushed ice, mint sprig
Method
  1. 1.Lightly muddle the mint with sugar syrup. Bruise it, do not shred it.
  2. 2.Pack a julep cup or highball tightly with crushed ice.
  3. 3.Pour the rum over the ice.
  4. 4.Stir until the outside of the glass frosts.
  5. 5.Top with more crushed ice and garnish with a mint sprig.
Spiced Rum Old Fashioned
A proper stirred-down serve · 5 mins
Ingredients
50mlExpedition Spiced Rum
1 tspDemerara sugar or sugar syrup
2 dashesAngostura bitters
To serveLarge ice cube, orange peel
Method
  1. 1.Add the sugar and bitters to a rocks glass.
  2. 2.Add a splash of water and stir until the sugar dissolves.
  3. 3.Place a large ice cube in the glass and pour in the rum.
  4. 4.Stir for 30 seconds until properly chilled.
  5. 5.Express the orange peel over the glass, rub it around the rim and drop it in.
